## Meaning — favored reading: a Samsung Internet (web) trigger, not an installed app

With high confidence the **enabling event sits inside this window**: clean
before, automated cascade running by the end. The total silence is itself
diagnostic — and a dense-capture check now tips *which* explanation it favors.

**Dense-capture check (2026-05-28).** The live scrape spans 2026-04-29→05-26 at
**37–67 events/day**; across the whole pre-cascade week (05-08→05-17) the only
non-mainstream apps active are three trivial junk leaves
(`com.exactaweather.liveweather`, two alarm clocks) — **all quiet before 05-18,
none declaring accessibility**. So **no controller-profile app is visible** in
the >2-week run-up. A *resident* seed that ever ran would risk eventually
emitting a Play "Used" event across that window; web content never would — so
the total absence **favors a non-app trigger**.

**Favored: the problem was initiated in Samsung Internet (web), not an app.** She
demonstrably uses Samsung Internet, whose history is **not in Google Takeout**. A
malvertising page / ad-network tapped there at ~07:09 fires the `market://`
deep-link storm and the Yahoo search-redirect with **no installed seed required**
— exactly why nothing shows pre-05-18. The "silent window" is then unmysterious:
off-the-record browsing, not a hidden app.

**superhomescreen makes it worse, but does not initiate it.** The symptoms that
*do* need an installed app — popups over other apps, "Settings closes itself" —
are covered by `com.sunteame.superhomescreen` (the only residue app declaring
accessibility + overlay), which is **visible mid-cascade** (Visited 07:16, ran by
10:43, automation signature from ~11:00). A *secondary* controller that escalated
the problem, not the morning trigger
(`../open-questions.kb/patient-zero-candidates.md`).

**Lesser alternative (not excluded):** a headless, sideloaded resident seed
(no foreground UI → no "Used"; no Play listing → no "Visited") could also evade
the log. Possible, but the dense-capture absence makes it the weaker reading.
